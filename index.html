TALKGS: HIGH-FIDELITY TALKING HEAD SYNTHESIS VIA TEMPORALLY CONSISTENT GAUSSIAN SPLATTING

TalkGS Framework

Abstract

Audio-driven 3D talking head synthesis is a challenging task. Existing radiance-based methods require long training times and often suffer from color blurring. Gaussian Splatting based methods also struggle with inaccurate lip movements and loss of fine facial details. We propose TalkGS, a high-fidelity talking head synthesis method based on temporally consistent Gaussian splatting.

Specifically, we adopt a two-stage training paradigm. The first stage performs person-agnostic generic training to capture cross-identity audio–motion mappings, while the second stage conducts identity-adaptive training to refine personalized motion based on the learned generic motion. To enhance the consistency of audio-motion mapping, we introduce a MotionFormer module, which encodes Gaussian representations over temporal windows to improve appearance consistency. MotionFormer is applied to the personalized motion module in both stages.

In addition, we design a patch-level geometric regularization to improve color fidelity and preserve facial details. Extensive experimental results demonstrate that TalkGS outperforms state-of-the-art methods in terms of synthesis quality, audio-motion synchronization, and visual fidelity.
