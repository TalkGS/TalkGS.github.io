GSTALK: FEW-SHOT TALKING HEAD SYNTHESIS BASED ON GAUSSIAN SPLATTING

Paper arXiv Code

GSTalk Framework

Abstract

Radiance field-based methods perform well in constructing 3D talking heads, but they require long training times and lack accurate reconstruction of facial texture details. We propose GSTalk, a 3D Gaussian Splatting-based reconstruction method to generate posable talking heads, addressing challenges such as reconstruction quality and audio-visual synchronization. Specifically, we design a universal head synthesizer that learns universal motion priors from a video corpus by decomposing personalized and universal motions. This stage is supervised using photometric image loss and negative correlation loss. We then rapidly construct identity-specific dynamic heads by combining individual motion fields with pre-trained universal motion fields, achieving highly accurate reconstruction results supervised by photometric loss and geometric constraints. Experimental results demonstrate that our method outperforms previous methods in terms of facial fidelity, lip sync accuracy, and rendering speed.
